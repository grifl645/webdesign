The Road Not Taken

Two roads diverged in a yellow wood,

  1. And be one traveler, long I stood
  2. And looked down one as far as I could
  3. To where it bent in the undergrowth;

Then took the other, as just as fair,

  1. And having perhaps the better claim,
  2. Because it was grassy and wanted wear;
  3. Though as for that the passing there
  4. Had worn them really about the same,

And both that morning equally lay

  1. And having perhaps the better claim,
  2. In leaves no step had trodden black.
  3. Oh, I kept the first for another day!
  4. Yet knowing how way leads on to way,
  5. I doubted if I should ever come back.
